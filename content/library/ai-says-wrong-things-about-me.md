# I Googled My Own Business and AI Said Something Completely Wrong About Me

You asked ChatGPT about your business. Or you Googled yourself and the AI Overview at the top of the results said something inaccurate. Wrong hours. Wrong services. A description that doesn't match what you actually do. Maybe it confused you with another business entirely.

It's a jarring feeling. You've spent years building your reputation, and now an AI is confidently telling thousands of people the wrong thing about you. And unlike a bad Google review, there's no "report" button or customer service line to call.

But it's fixable. You just have to understand where AI gets its information and start correcting it at the source.

## Where does AI get its information about my business?

AI models don't have a reporter on staff fact-checking your business. They pull from publicly available data across the web. The main sources:

**Your website.** This is the most important one. Whatever your website says about your business is what AI is most likely to repeat. If your website is outdated, incomplete, or confusing, the AI's understanding of your business will be too.

**Google Business Profile.** Your GBP listing is a primary source for local business information. Hours, location, categories, services, and description all get picked up by AI tools.

**Business directories.** Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages, industry-specific directories, chamber of commerce listings. AI crawls these to build a picture of your business. If your information is inconsistent across directories, AI gets confused.

**Social media profiles.** Your Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn business pages are all public data that AI can reference.

**Review sites.** AI reads reviews to understand what customers say about your business. If 50 reviews mention "great pizza" but you're actually a Mediterranean restaurant that happens to serve pizza, AI might characterize you primarily as a pizza place.

**News articles and press mentions.** If you've been mentioned in local news or blogs, that content becomes part of AI's understanding of your business.

**Cached and outdated data.** Here's the frustrating part: AI models are trained on data snapshots. The information they have might be months or even a year old. Even if you updated your website yesterday, the AI might still be working from a cached version of your old site.

## Why is the information often wrong or outdated?

A few reasons:

**Data lag.** AI models are trained on data from a specific point in time. They don't browse the internet in real-time (though some newer tools like Perplexity do real-time search). If you changed your hours, moved locations, or added services since the last training data update, the AI won't know.

**Conflicting sources.** If your website says you close at 8 PM, your Google listing says 9 PM, and Yelp says 7 PM, the AI has to pick one — and it might pick wrong. Inconsistency across platforms is the number one cause of AI inaccuracy for small businesses.

**Thin or unclear website content.** If your website is mostly images and doesn't clearly describe your services, location, or business model in text, AI has to guess. It'll pull from whatever text it can find, and that might be a directory listing with incomplete information.

**Entity confusion.** If another business has a similar name, operates in the same city, or is in the same industry, AI can mix the two of you together. This is especially common for businesses with generic names.

## How do I audit what AI is saying about me?

Before you start fixing things, you need to know what's broken. Run what I call an AI readability audit:

1. **Google your business name.** Read the AI Overview if one appears. Note any inaccuracies.
2. **Ask ChatGPT about your business.** Use your full business name and city. "Tell me about [Business Name] in [City, State]." Note what it gets right and wrong.
3. **Try Perplexity.** Same question. Perplexity cites its sources, so you can see exactly where it's pulling from.
4. **Ask from a customer's perspective.** "What are the best [your service] providers in [your city]?" See if you show up and what it says about you.
5. **Check your competitor too.** See what AI says about them. If their description is more accurate and detailed, look at why — they probably have better source material.

Document everything. What's accurate, what's wrong, and what's missing. This gives you a clear list of what needs to be fixed and where.

## How do I fix what AI says about my business?

You can't edit AI directly. What you can do is fix the sources it pulls from. When AI re-crawls or retrains on updated data, it'll pick up the corrections. Here's where to focus:

**Fix your website first.** This is your primary source of truth. Make sure these things are clearly stated in text (not just in images or PDFs):

- Your exact business name
- Your full address and service area
- Your current hours of operation
- A clear description of every service you offer
- Your phone number and email
- Your founding year and any key facts AI should know

Write a comprehensive "About" page that reads like a business profile. Include the kind of information you'd want AI to have about you. Be specific: "We've been serving the Austin area since 2012" is better than "We've been in business for years."

**Update and complete your Google Business Profile.** Every field. Every service. Accurate hours. Accurate categories. Upload recent photos. Write a thorough business description. This is the single most impactful thing you can do because Google's AI Overviews pull directly from GBP data.

**Audit your directory listings.** Check Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages, Apple Maps, Facebook, and any industry-specific directories. Make sure your name, address, phone number, and services are identical across all of them. If your business is listed as "Smith & Sons Plumbing" on your website but "Smith and Sons Plumbing LLC" on Yelp, that inconsistency causes problems.

**Update your social media profiles.** Make sure your business bio, services, hours, and contact information are current on every platform. These profiles are easy to set and forget, but AI reads them.

## What is entity consistency and why does it matter?

Entity consistency means that every mention of your business across the internet tells the same story. Same name, same address, same phone number, same services, same description.

When AI encounters consistent information from 10 different sources, it develops high confidence in that information. When it encounters conflicting information, confidence drops and it either picks a random version or hedges with vague language.

The simplest way to think about it: if an alien landed on Earth and could only learn about your business by reading the internet, would they get a clear, accurate picture? Or would they be confused by contradictions?

Make your NAP (name, address, phone number) identical everywhere. Use the same business name format. If you're "Joe's Auto Shop" on your website, don't be "Joe's Automotive" on Facebook and "Joe's Auto Repair Shop" on Yelp. Pick one and use it everywhere.

## How does schema markup help correct AI's understanding?

Schema markup is structured data you add to your website's code that explicitly labels your business information for search engines and AI tools. It's like filling out a form that says "Here's my business name. Here's my address. Here are my hours. Here are my services."

Without schema, AI has to read your website like a human and interpret what it finds. With schema, you're handing it the answers in a format it can process instantly and accurately.

The most useful schema types for correcting AI information:

- **LocalBusiness schema.** Your foundational business data — name, address, phone, hours, geo-coordinates, service area.
- **Service schema.** Each service you offer, with descriptions.
- **FAQ schema.** Common questions and answers about your business, marked up so AI can extract them directly.
- **Organization schema.** Your founding date, logo, social media links, and other organizational details.

If you use WordPress, plugins like Rank Math or Yoast can generate schema for you. If you have a developer, ask them to implement LocalBusiness schema at minimum. It's a one-time effort that significantly improves how accurately AI understands and represents your business.

## How long does it take for corrections to show up?

This is the frustrating part: it's not instant. Different AI systems update at different speeds.

- **Google AI Overviews** can reflect changes within days to weeks, especially if you update your Google Business Profile.
- **Perplexity** does real-time search, so it picks up website changes relatively quickly.
- **ChatGPT and Claude** train on data snapshots. Changes to your website might not be reflected for months until the next training data update. However, newer versions with web search capabilities can pick up current information.

The best strategy is to fix your sources now and be patient. The corrections will propagate. In the meantime, keep your website and profiles accurate and consistent — it's good for traditional search and for humans who visit your site directly.

## The takeaway

You can't edit AI, but you can control the sources it learns from. Start with an AI audit to understand what's being said about you. Then fix your website, update your Google Business Profile, clean up your directory listings, and implement schema markup. Make your information consistent everywhere. It takes some upfront effort, but once your source material is accurate and consistent, AI will eventually get the picture right — and so will your potential customers.
