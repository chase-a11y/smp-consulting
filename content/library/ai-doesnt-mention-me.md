# People Are Asking AI for Recommendations and My Business Never Comes Up

More and more people are skipping Google entirely and asking ChatGPT, Google's AI Overview, Perplexity, or Siri for recommendations. "What's the best yoga studio in Scottsdale?" "Find me a good accountant for freelancers." "Who should I call for a roof repair in Dallas?"

If your business never shows up in those answers, you've got a growing problem. AI-powered search isn't coming — it's here. And the businesses that show up in AI-generated answers are pulling ahead in a way that traditional SEO alone can't keep up with.

This is a new playing field, but the rules aren't as mysterious as they seem.

## What is AEO and why should I care?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. It's the practice of structuring your online presence so that AI tools can find, understand, and cite your business when answering someone's question.

Traditional SEO is about ranking on a list of 10 blue links. AEO is about being the answer. When someone asks an AI "who's the best family dentist in Portland," the AI doesn't return a list of websites. It gives a direct answer, sometimes naming specific businesses. If your business isn't in the sources the AI draws from, it can't recommend you. Period.

This matters because search behavior is changing fast. Younger consumers especially are using AI assistants as their primary discovery tool. If you're only optimized for traditional Google search, you're optimizing for a shrinking piece of the pie.

## Why does AI recommend some businesses and not others?

AI models like ChatGPT and Google's Gemini don't have secret partnerships with local businesses. They generate answers based on the information available to them, which comes from:

- **Publicly accessible web content.** If your website has clear, well-structured information about who you are, what you do, and where you do it, AI can find it and use it.
- **Aggregated review data.** AI tools pull from platforms like Google Reviews, Yelp, and industry-specific review sites. Businesses with more reviews, higher ratings, and detailed customer feedback are more likely to be mentioned.
- **Structured data (schema markup).** This is code on your website that explicitly tells machines what your business is. Think of it as a machine-readable business card.
- **Third-party mentions.** If your business is mentioned on other websites — local news, industry blogs, directories, "best of" lists — AI has more sources to draw from when building an answer.
- **Entity consistency.** AI tools build an internal model of your business as an "entity." If your business name, address, services, and other details are consistent across every source, the AI builds a stronger, more confident entity profile. Inconsistency creates doubt.

The theme here is the same as SEO but amplified: clarity, consistency, and credibility. AI tools need to be confident that information is accurate before they'll cite it.

## What is structured data and how does it help with AI?

Structured data (also called schema markup) is a standardized format for providing information about your business directly in your website's code. It uses a vocabulary from schema.org that search engines and AI tools understand natively.

For a local business, the most important schema types are:

- **LocalBusiness** (or a more specific subtype like Restaurant, DentalClinic, LegalService)
- **Service** — describes what you offer
- **Review / AggregateRating** — your review data
- **FAQPage** — your frequently asked questions
- **Organization** — your business entity details

When you add this markup to your site, you're not relying on Google or ChatGPT to figure out what your business is by reading your paragraphs. You're spelling it out in a format they're designed to consume. It's the difference between handing someone a well-organized file folder versus dumping a box of papers on their desk.

If your website doesn't have structured data, you should add it. Most modern website platforms (WordPress, Squarespace, Wix) have plugins or built-in tools for this. If you're on a custom site, your developer can add it using JSON-LD format, which is Google's preferred method.

## How do I check if AI can "see" my business right now?

Here's a simple test I call the AI readability audit. It takes about 5 minutes:

1. **Ask ChatGPT about your business.** Type: "Tell me about [your business name] in [your city]." See what it says. Is the information accurate? Is it mentioned at all? If the AI says it doesn't have information about you, that's your baseline.

2. **Ask for a recommendation in your category.** Type: "What are the best [your service] in [your city]?" See if you're mentioned. Note who is mentioned and why the AI seems to favor them.

3. **Paste your homepage content into ChatGPT.** Ask: "Based on this text, what does this business do, where is it located, and who is it for?" If the AI can't clearly answer those three questions from your homepage, neither can any other AI that crawls your site.

4. **Check Google's AI Overview.** Search for your service + city on Google. If an AI Overview appears at the top of results, see if your business or website is cited as a source.

This audit tells you how AI currently perceives your business. If the results are bad, that's actually useful information — it means you know what to fix.

## How do I make my content citable by AI?

AI tools extract and cite content that is clear, specific, and well-organized. Here's how to write content that AI can easily pull from:

**Lead with the answer.** Don't bury your key information at the bottom of a paragraph. Start with the direct answer, then explain. This mirrors how AI constructs responses — it looks for concise, definitive statements.

**Use question-based headings.** Structure your pages and blog posts with H2 headings that match real questions people ask. "How much does teeth whitening cost?" is more AI-friendly than "Our Whitening Services."

**Be specific.** "We serve the greater Phoenix metro area including Scottsdale, Tempe, Mesa, and Chandler" is infinitely more useful to AI than "We serve the local area." Names, places, numbers, and specifics give AI concrete data to work with.

**Create FAQ content.** A well-structured FAQ page (with proper FAQPage schema markup) is one of the easiest wins for AEO. Each question-answer pair is a self-contained unit that AI can extract and use directly.

**Maintain a consistent entity.** Your business name, founder names, location, founding year, services, and other factual details should appear consistently across your website, Google Business Profile, social media profiles, and directory listings. AI cross-references these sources. Consistency builds confidence.

## What can I do this month to start showing up in AI answers?

AEO is still a young discipline, so the businesses that start now have a real advantage. Here's a practical starting plan:

1. **Run the AI readability audit** described above. Document what AI currently knows (or doesn't know) about your business.

2. **Update your website's "About" page** to clearly state, in plain language: what your business is, what you do, who you serve, and where you're located. Make it factual and specific.

3. **Add structured data** to your website. At minimum, add LocalBusiness schema with your name, address, phone, hours, and services.

4. **Create or improve your FAQ page.** Write 10 to 15 real questions your customers ask, with clear, concise answers. Add FAQPage schema markup.

5. **Get mentioned on third-party sites.** Aim for at least 3 new mentions this month — a local business directory, a chamber of commerce listing, a guest post on a local blog, a "best of" list. Each mention gives AI another source to draw from.

6. **Keep your reviews flowing.** AI tools weigh review data heavily. Keep asking for reviews on Google and respond to every one.

## The takeaway

AI-driven search is not a future trend. It's happening right now, and it's reshaping how customers find businesses. The good news is that the fundamentals haven't changed — you still need clear information, consistent presence, and genuine credibility. What's changed is the format. AI needs your business information to be structured, specific, and easy to extract. The businesses that adapt to this now will be the ones that AI recommends for years to come. The ones that ignore it will wonder why the phone stopped ringing.
