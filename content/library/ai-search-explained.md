# I Don't Understand What AI Search Means for My Business

You keep hearing that AI is changing search. Google has AI Overviews now. People are using ChatGPT and Perplexity to find businesses and answers instead of typing into a traditional search engine. Everyone says you need to "optimize for AI" but nobody explains what that means in practical terms.

If you're a small business owner trying to understand whether this is something you need to worry about or act on, here's the straightforward version: AI is changing how people find information, but for local businesses, the fundamentals haven't changed as much as the headlines suggest. In some ways, the shift is actually an opportunity.

Let me break it down.

## What is AI search and how is it different from regular Google?

Traditional search works like this: you type a question, Google shows you a list of 10 links, and you click through them to find your answer. You're doing the work of reading multiple pages and deciding what's relevant.

AI search flips that. Instead of giving you links, it reads those pages for you and gives you a synthesized answer directly. Google's AI Overviews do this right at the top of the search results page. Tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Copilot do it as their entire product — you ask a question, they give you an answer compiled from multiple sources.

For the person searching, it's faster and more convenient. For businesses, it changes the game because your website might be the source of an AI answer without the person ever clicking through to visit your site. Or your site might not be referenced at all, even if you rank well in traditional search.

## What does AEO mean and why are people talking about it?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. It's the practice of structuring your content so that AI tools can easily extract and cite your information when generating answers.

Traditional SEO is about ranking — getting your page to position 1, 2, or 3 on Google. AEO is about being cited — having your content be the source that AI pulls from when answering a question.

The good news is that most AEO best practices overlap with good SEO. Clear writing, question-based headings, concise answers near the top of your content, and well-structured data on your website. If you're already doing solid SEO work, you're most of the way there.

The main shift is in how you structure content. Instead of writing long, meandering paragraphs that eventually get to the point, you want to lead with clear, direct answers that an AI can extract. Think about how you'd answer a question if someone asked you face to face — you'd give the answer first, then explain the details. Write your website content the same way.

## What does this mean for local businesses specifically?

Here's where it gets interesting, and where I think the news is mostly good for small businesses.

**Local search is still heavily map- and profile-based.** When someone searches "plumber near me" or "best pizza in [city]," Google still leans heavily on Google Business Profiles, reviews, and proximity. AI hasn't replaced the map pack. Your Google Business Profile is still your most important local search asset.

**AI tools are citing local businesses.** When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best HVAC company in Austin," it pulls from review sites, directories, and business websites. If your business has a strong online presence — good reviews, an informative website, consistent directory listings — you're more likely to be mentioned.

**Voice search and conversational search benefit clear content.** More people are asking their phones and smart speakers full questions instead of typing keywords. "Where can I get my car detailed on a Saturday?" instead of "car detailing Saturday." If your website answers those kinds of questions clearly, you're in a good position.

**The "zero-click" concern is real but manageable.** Yes, some people will get their answer from an AI overview and never visit your site. But for local businesses, the intent is almost always transactional — they're looking for a place to go or a service to hire. They still need to contact you, visit you, or book with you. The AI answer is more like a recommendation than a replacement.

## What is structured data and should I care about it?

Structured data (also called schema markup) is code on your website that explicitly tells search engines and AI tools what your content means. Instead of making Google guess that "123 Main Street" is your business address, structured data labels it as your address. Instead of guessing your hours, it labels them as hours.

For small businesses, the most important types of structured data are:

- **LocalBusiness schema.** Your name, address, phone number, hours, and service area.
- **FAQ schema.** If you have a FAQ page, this markup tells AI exactly which questions you answer and what the answers are. This is one of the most direct ways to get cited in AI answers.
- **Review schema.** Marks up your reviews and ratings so they can appear as rich results.
- **Service schema.** Describes the specific services you offer.

If this sounds technical, it is — but most website platforms (WordPress, Squarespace, Wix) have plugins or built-in tools that handle it for you. If you work with a web developer, ask them to implement local business schema and FAQ schema at minimum. It's a one-time setup that pays dividends.

## How do I go from ranking to being cited?

Here's the practical shift. With traditional SEO, success means people click on your link. With AI search, success means your content is the source the AI references when answering a question.

To increase your chances of being cited:

**Answer questions directly.** Use question-based headings (like this article does) and provide clear, concise answers in the first sentence or two after the heading. AI tools love content that follows a question-and-answer structure.

**Be the most useful source.** AI tools tend to pull from content that's comprehensive, specific, and authoritative. A page that thoroughly explains "how much does a kitchen remodel cost in Denver" with real numbers and breakdowns is more likely to be cited than a page that vaguely says "contact us for a quote."

**Keep your information current.** AI tools weigh freshness. If your website hasn't been updated in two years, it's less likely to be cited than a competitor's site with recent content.

**Maintain entity consistency.** Your business name, location, services, and other key details should be consistent across your website, Google Business Profile, social media profiles, and directory listings. When AI encounters the same information from multiple sources, it increases confidence in citing it.

## Should I be worried about AI search?

Not worried, but aware. The shift is gradual, and local businesses are less affected than informational content sites. Here's the honest breakdown:

**What hasn't changed:** People still need to find, contact, and visit local businesses. Reviews still matter. Google Business Profiles still matter. Having a clear, informative website still matters.

**What has changed:** How people arrive at the decision. Instead of clicking through three websites and comparing, they might get an AI-generated recommendation and go straight to the winner. That makes your online presence — reviews, website content, directory consistency — even more important, because the AI is doing the comparison on their behalf.

**What to do about it:** Focus on the fundamentals. Accurate, comprehensive information on your website. Strong reviews. Consistent directory listings. Structured data markup. Clear, question-and-answer-style content. These things help you in traditional search and AI search simultaneously.

## The takeaway

AI search isn't something to panic about. For most local businesses, it's a continuation of the same game with slightly different rules. The businesses that show up in AI answers are the ones with clear, accurate, well-structured information across the web. Focus on making your online presence the most trustworthy, comprehensive, and up-to-date version of your business story — and the AI will find you.
