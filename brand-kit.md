# The Merchant Guide — Brand Kit

## What we are
A consulting practice for startups and small businesses. We handle payments, digital marketing, and operations so founders can focus on talking to their customers.

## Brand personality
Warm, approachable, honest, human. Think walking into a good independent coffee shop — not a corporate office, not a tech startup. We're the experienced friend who actually knows what they're talking about.

## Tone
Conversational, confident but not salesy. Plain language over jargon. We speak like a trusted advisor, not an agency pitch deck.

## Color palette
- Paper/background: #f7f1de (warm cream)
- Cream: #f3e9c8 (slightly deeper warm cream)
- Amber (primary accent): #d4a04a (golden amber — the signature color)
- Amber deep: #b07f2c (darker amber for depth)
- Olive/sage: #5d6240 (earthy green, used sparingly)
- Ink: #1f1d14 (warm near-black for text)
- Ink 2: #3a3526 (warm dark brown for secondary text)
- Muted: #6c6750 (warm gray-brown for captions)
- Rust: #b15a32 (occasional warm accent)

## Typography
- Headings & body: Geist Sans (clean, modern, friendly)
- Labels, eyebrows, mono details: Geist Mono (monospace, editorial feel)
- Style: editorial but warm — not newspaper-cold, more like a well-designed indie magazine

## Visual style
- Clean line art / editorial illustration
- Warm earth tones — creams, ambers, soft browns, muted sage
- Minimal and uncluttered — let the warmth come from color and tone, not busyness
- Subtle texture is fine (paper grain, soft shadows) but nothing heavy or grungy
- Animations should feel gentle and natural — soft easing, slow loops, nothing flashy

## Imagery themes
- Small storefronts, open signs, countertops
- Handshakes, conversations, people across tables
- Handwritten notes, pens, coffee cups
- Growing plants, seedlings (trust/growth metaphor)
- Simple charts trending up, receipts, checklists
- Main Street, not Silicon Valley

## Do not
- Use corporate stock photo energy (suits, glass buildings, forced smiles)
- Use neon, gradient-heavy, or tech-bro aesthetics
- Make it feel like a SaaS landing page
- Use cold blues, purples, or high-saturation colors
- Overcomplicate — simplicity is the brand
